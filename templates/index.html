COMMON SICKNESS
THAT OCCURS IN THE HEAD

  • Migraine
    • -Severe headaches
    -Nausea
    -Vomiting
    -Sensitivity to light and sound

  • Tension headaches
    • -Mild to moderate headaches
    -Pressure like pain in the forehead, temples, or the back of the head

  • Sinusitis
    • -Headache
    -Facial pain
    -Nasal congestion
    -Thick green or yellow discharge from the nose

  • Concussion
    • -Headache
    -Confusion
    -Memory loss
    -Dizziness
    -Blurred Vision

  • Bells Palsy
    • -Sudden weakness or paralysis of the muscles on one side of the face
    -Drooling
    -Difficulty smiling or closing the eye on the side

  • Meniere's Disease
    • -Vertigo
    -Ringing in the ear (Tinnitus)
    -Hearing loss
    -Feeling of fullness in ear

  • Acoustic Neuroma
    • -Gradual hearing loss
    -Ringing in the ear
    -Dizziness
    -Hearing loss

  • Stroke
    • -Sudden numbness or weakness on one side of the face or body
    -Trouble speaking or understanding speech
    -Confusion
    -Loss of balance or coordination

  • Trigeminal Neuralgia
    • -Sudden severe facial pain
    -Usually triggered by simple activities such as:
    a. Eating
    b. Speaking
    c. Brushing of teeth

  • Depression
    • -Persistent feeling of:
    a. Sadness
    b. Hopelesness
    c. Worthlesness
    -Changes in apettite and sleeping patterns
    -Lack of interest in activities once enjoyed.

COMMON SICKNESS
THAT OCCURS IN THE MIDDLE PART OF THE BODY

  • Diabetes
    • -Excessive thirst
    -Frequent urination
    -Unexplained weight loss
    -Fatigue
    -Blurry vision

  • Arthritis
    • -Joint pain
    -Stiffness
    -Swelling particularly in:
    a. Hand
    b. Feet
    c. Wrists
    d. Knee
    e. Hips

  • Heart Disease
    • -Chest pain
    -Shortness of breath
    -Lightheadness
    -Fatigue

  • Cancer
    • -Fatigue
    -Unexplained weight loss
    -Persistent pain
    -Changes in skin or mole appearance
    -Persistent coughing or change in voice

  • Asthma
    • -Shortness of breath
    -Wheezing
    -Coughing
    -Chest tightness

  • Kidney Disease
    • -Fatigue
    -Swelling in the legs, ankle, and feet
    -Changes in urination frequency and appearance
    -Persistent itching

  • Liver Disease
    • -Fatigue
    -Abdominal pain and swelling
    -Jaundice (Yellowing of the skin and eyes, itchy skin)

  • Thyroid Disorders
    • -Fatigue
    -Weight changes
    -Sensitivity to cold or heat
    -Changes in skin and hair texture
    -Changes in menstrual cycles

  • Influenza (Flu)
    • -Sudden fever
    -Headaches
    -Muscle Aches
    -Fatigue
    -Runny or stuffy nose

  • Irritable Bowel Syndrome (IBS)
    • -Abdominal pain
    -Bloating
    -Constipation
    -Diarrhea

  • Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)
    • -Heart burn
    -Acid regurgitation
    -Difficulty swallowing

COMMON SICKNESS
THAT OCCURS IN THE LOWER PART OF THE BODY

  • Osteoarthritis
    • -Joint pain
    -Stifness and swelling particularly in:
    a. Hips
    b. Kness
    c. Lower back

  • Sciatica
    • -Sharp pain in the lower back and leg
    -Numbness
    -Tingling or weakness in the leg

  • Varicose Vein
    • -Swollen, twisted veins usually in the legs causing:
    a. Pain
    b. Swelling
    c. Aching

  • Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT)
    • -Swelling and pain in one leg
    -Redness and warmth in the affected area

  • Prostate Issues
    • -Frequent urination
    -Difficulty starting or stopping urination
    -Weak urine flow
    -Pain or burning sensation during urination

  • Urinary Incontinence
    • -Loss of bladder control
    -Leaking urine unexpectedly

  • Endometriosis
    • -Pelvic pain
    -Heavy menstrual periods
    -Pain during intercourse and fatigue

  • Hemorrhoids
    • -Itching, pain, and bleeding during bowel movements
    -Bulging or tender lump near the anus
