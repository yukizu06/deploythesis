Symptoms for Diseases



  • ALLERGOLOGY
    • Asthma
    -Wheezing
    -Chest tightness
    -Shortness of breath
    -Coughing
    -Rapid breathing
    -Difficulty breathing
    -Chest pain or pressure

    Allergic rhinitis (hay fever)
    -Sneezing
    -Runny or stuffy nose
    -Itching
    -Watery eyes
    -Dark circles under the eyes
    -Coughing
    -Fatigue
    -Headache
    -Itchy palate

    Urticaria (hives)
    -Raised, red welts
    -Itching
    -Swelling
    -Flushing
    -Tingling

  • CARDIOLOGY
    • Coronary artery disease (CAD)
    -Chest pain or discomfort
    -Shortness of breath
    -Fatigue
    -Lightheadedness or dizziness
    -Rapid or irregular heartbeat
    -Sweating
    -Nausea
    -Arm or jaw pain

    Heart failure
    -Shortness of breath
    -Fatigue
    -Swelling
    -Rapid or irregular heartbeat
    -Chest pain or discomfort
    -Persistent coughing or wheezing
    -Reduced ability to exercise
    -Decreased appetite or weight gain

    Hypertension (high blood pressure)
    -Headache
    -Dizziness
    -Chest pain
    -Shortness of breath
    -Rapid or irregular heartbeat
    -Blurred vision
    -Nausea or vomiting
    -Nosebleeds

    Pulmonary embolism
    -Shortness of breath
    -Chest pain
    -Rapid or irregular heartbeat
    -Sweating
    -Coughing
    -Lightheadedness or dizziness
    -Fainting
    -Swelling

  • DENTAL
    • Periodontal disease
    -Bleeding gums
    -Red, swollen, or tender gums
    -Receding gums
    -Loose or shifting teeth
    -Persistent bad breath or bad taste in the mouth
    -Pus between the gums and teeth
    -Changes in bite or fit of dentures

    Toothache
    -Sharp or dull pain in the affected tooth
    -Sensitivity to hot or cold temperatures
    -Sweet sensitivity
    -Pain when biting or chewing
    -Swelling in the area around the affected tooth
    -Tenderness in the area around the affected tooth
    -Ear pain

    Temporomandibular joint (TMJ) disorder
    -Pain or tenderness in the jaw
    -Popping or clicking sounds in the jaw
    -Difficulty or pain when opening or closing the mouth
    -Lockjaw
    -Jaw pain or stiffness
    -Tension headaches
    -Dizziness or vertigo

    Dry mouth
    -Thirst
    -Sticky, dry feeling in the mouth
    -Cracked or dry lips
    -Sore throat
    -Difficulty speaking
    -Difficulty eating or swallowing
    -Bad breath
    -Changes in taste

  • DERMATOLOGY
    • Acne
    -Blackheads
    -Whiteheads
    -Pimples
    -Cysts
    -Papules
    -Nodules
    -Scars
    -Oily skin

    Atopic dermatitis (eczema)
    -Dry, scaly, and itchy skin
    -Rash
    -Bumps
    -Blisters
    -Thickened skin
    -Darkened skin
    -Lichenification

    Alopecia (hair loss)
    -Gradual or sudden hair thinning
    -Bald patches
    -Receding hairline
    -Loss of body hair
    -Short, broken hairs
    -Round or oval bald patches

    Melanoma (skin cancer)
    -New or changing moles
    -Asymmetrical moles
    -Irregular border
    -Uneven color
    -Diameter larger than a pencil eraser
    -Itching, pain, or bleeding
    -Dark or black spots

    Fungal skin infections (such as ringworm or athlete's foot)
    -Rash
    -Blisters
    -Scaling or flaking skin
    -Itching:
    -Burning or stinging
    -Discoloration
    -Cracking or peeling skin

    Bacterial skin infections (such as impetigo)
    -Blisters
    -Rashes
    -Pus-filled sores
    -Swelling
    -Pain
    -Warmth or redness
    -Crusting or scabbing

    Viral skin infections (such as shingles or warts)
    -Blisters
    -Rash
    -Itching
    -Pain
    -Discoloration

    Diabetes
    -Increased thirst and frequent urination
    -Hunger, even after eating
    -Blurred vision
    -Fatigue
    -Slow wound healing
    -Numbness or tingling in the feet and hands
    -Recurrent infections, such as yeast infections or skin infections

  • GASTROENTEROLOGY
    • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
    -Heartburn
    -Regurgitation
    -Chest pain
    -Dry cough
    -Difficulty swallowing
    -Sore throat
    -Asthma symptoms
    -Hoarse voice
    -Bad breath
    -Nausea

    Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS)
    -Abdominal pain or cramping
    -Diarrhea
    -Constipation
    -Bloating
    -Gas
    -Mucus in the stool
    -Urgency
    -Altered bowel movements
    -Abdominal distension
    -Rectal discomfort

    Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD)
    -Abdominal pain
    -Diarrhea
    -Blood in the stool
    -Weight loss
    -Fatigue
    -Anemia
    -Fever
    -Joint pain
    -Skin lesions
    -Abdominal distension

    Peptic ulcer disease
    -Abdominal pain
    -Heartburn
    -Nausea
    -Bloating
    -Loss of appetite
    -Weight loss
    -Fatigue
    -Black or tarry stools
    -Vomiting blood

    Hepatitis
    -Fatigue
    -Nausea
    -Loss of appetite
    -Abdominal pain
    -Dark urine
    -Clay-colored stools
    -Jaundice
    -Itching
    -Fever

    Pancreatitis
    -Abdominal pain
    -Nausea and vomiting
    -Fever
    -Rapid pulse
    -Sweating
    -Dehydration
    -Diarrhea
    -Weight loss
    -Jaundice

    Gastroparesis
    -Nausea
    -Vomiting
    -Abdominal bloating
    -Abdominal pain
    -Acid reflux
    -Weight loss
    -Early satiety
    -Poor blood sugar control in diabetics

    Colorectal cancer
    -Change in bowel habits
    -Rectal bleeding
    -Abdominal pain or cramping
    -Weakness or fatigue
    -Unexplained weight loss
    -Anemia
    -Gas or bloating

  • HEMATOLOGY
    • Anemia
    -Fatigue and weakness
    -Pale skin
    -Rapid heartbeat
    -Shortness of breath
    -Dizziness or lightheadedness
    -Cold hands and feet
    -Brittle nails
    -Headache
    -Irritability or difficulty concentrating

    Hemophilia
    -Excessive bleeding
    -Unexpected or spontaneous bleeding
    -Large or deep bruises
    -Joint pain and swelling
    -Prolonged bleeding after injury or surgery
    -Nosebleeds
    -Bloody gums or mouth

    Leukemia
    -fatigue
    -weakness
    -skin
    -easy bruising or bleeding
    -frequent infections
    -bone pain or tenderness
    -swollen lymph nodes
    -loss of appetite
    -weight loss
    -fever
    -night sweats
    -shortness of breath

    Lymphoma
    -swollen lymph nodes
    -fatigue, fever
    -night sweats
    -weight loss
    -loss of appetite
    -itchy skin
    -painless lumps in the neck
    -underarm
    -stomach or groin

  • INFECTIOUS DISEASE
    • Influenza (the flu)
    -Fever
    -Cough
    -Sore throat
    -Runny or stuffy nose
    -Body or muscle aches
    -Headache
    -Fatigue (tiredness)

    Tuberculosis (TB)
    -Persistent cough lasting 3 or more weeks
    -Chest pain
    -Coughing up blood or sputum
    -Fatigue
    -Loss of appetite and weight loss
    -Chills and night sweats
    -Fever
    -Shortness of breath

    Human immunodeficiency virus (HIV)/Acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS)
    -Flu-like symptoms such as fever, fatigue, muscle aches, and headache
    -Sore throat
    -Rash
    -Night sweats
    -Swollen lymph nodes

    Malaria
    -Fever
    -Chills
    -Headache
    -Muscle pain
    -Fatigue
    -Nausea and vomiting
    -Sweats (often occurring at night)
    -Diarrhea
    -Anemia
    -Low blood pressure

    Hepatitis B and C
    -Fatigue
    -Nausea
    -Loss of appetite
    -Abdominal pain or discomfort
    -Dark urine
    -Clay-colored bowel movements
    -Joint pain
    -Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
    -Itching
    -Flu-like symptoms (fever, headache, muscle and joint aches)

    SARS-CoV-2 (the virus that causes COVID-19)
    -Fever
    -Dry cough
    -Fatigue
    -Shortness of breath
    -Body aches and muscle pain
    -Headache
    -Sore throat
    -Loss of taste or smell
    -Congestion or runny nose
    -Nausea or vomiting
    -Diarrhea

    Chickenpox
    -Fever
    -Fatigue
    -Headache
    -Sore throat
    -Loss of appetite
    -Itching
    -A rash of red spots
    -Body aches.
    -Nausea.

    Pneumonia
    -Cough, with or without mucus or phlegm
    -Chest pain or discomfort when breathing or coughing
    -Fatigue or weakness
    -Fever
    -Sweating or chills
    -Shortness of breath
    -Rapid breathing or rapid heart rate
    -Low appetite or nausea
    -Muscle or joint aches

  • NEPHROLOGY
    • Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)
    -Decreased urine output
    -Swelling in legs, ankles, and feet
    -Nausea and vomiting
    -Shortness of breath
    -Fatigue and weakness
    -Confusion
    -Chest pain
    -Seizures or muscle twitching (in severe cases)

    Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
    -Fatigue and weakness
    -Swelling in the feet, ankles, legs, face, and/or hands
    -Dry and itchy skin
    -Nausea and loss of appetite
    -Trouble sleeping
    -Muscle cramps or twitching
    -Changes in the amount of urine
    -Decreased mental sharpness
    -Shortness of breath
    -Chest pain or pressure
    -Irregular heartbeat
    -High blood pressure
    -Darkening of the skin, particularly on the neck or in the groin area
    -Unexpected weight loss or gain
    -Bone pain and tenderness
    -Numbness or tingling in the hands and feet

    Glomerulonephritis
    -Swelling in legs, ankles, or feet
    -High blood pressure
    -Foamy or bloody urine
    -Fatigue
    -Decreased urination
    -Itching skin
    -Nausea or vomiting
    -Muscle cramps or twitching
    -Rapid weight gain
    -Shortness of breath.

    Nephrotic Syndrome
    -Swelling in various parts of the body, such as face, eyes, ankles, legs, and/or abdomen
    -Foamy appearance of urine
    -Weight gain
    -Increased need to urinate during the night
    -Decreased urine output
    -Fatigue and/or weakness
    -Increased blood pressure
    -Proteinuria (excessive protein in urine)

    Polycystic Kidney Disease (PKD)
    -Abdominal pain or tenderness
    -Back or side pain
    -Enlarged or cyst-filled kidneys
    -High blood pressure
    -Kidney stones
    -Urinary tract infections
    -Blood in the urine (hematuria)
    -Decreased kidney function
    -Urinary urgency and frequency

    Urinary Tract Infections (UTI)
    -Pain or burning during urination
    -Frequent urge to urinate
    -Passing small amounts of urine
    -Strong, persistent urge to urinate
    -Passing frequent, small amounts of urine
    -Cloudy or strong-smelling urine
    -Urine that appears red, bright pink or brown, due to blood in the urine
    -Pain in the lower abdomen or back
    -Fatigue
    -Nausea
    -Vomiting

  • NEUROLOGY
    • Alzheimer's disease
    -Memory loss, especially short-term memory
    -Difficulty with language, such as finding the right words
    -Difficulty with routine tasks, such as getting dressed or brushing teeth
    -Confusion about time, place or person
    -Changes in mood or behavior, such as depression or anxiety
    -Difficulty with spatial orientation, such as getting lost in familiar places
    -Changes in personality or motivation
    -Paranoia, delusions or hallucinations.

    Parkinson's disease
    -Tremors or shaking in hands, arms, legs, jaw or head
    -Stiffness or rigidity of muscles
    -Slow movement and difficulty with initiating movement (bradykinesia)
    -Poor balance and coordination
    -Loss of automatic movements, such as blinking or smiling
    -Speech changes, including speaking softly or in a monotone voice
    -Writing difficulties
    -Depression and anxiety
    -Memory and cognitive changes
    -Constipation and other digestive problems.

    Epilepsy
    -Seizures
    -Temporary confusion
    -A staring spell
    -Uncontrollable jerking movements of the arms and legs
    -Loss of consciousness or awareness
    -Mental confusion
    -Strange feelings, such as fear or déjà vu
    -Sudden jerking movements of the body, limbs or face
    -Loss of bladder control or bowel function during a seizure.

    Migraines
    -Severe headache, usually on one side of the head
    -Throbbing or pulsing pain
    -Nausea and vomiting
    -Sensitivity to light and sound
    -Visual aura, such as flashing lights or blind spots
    -Fatigue and lack of energy
    -Stiff neck
    -Mood changes, such as irritability or depression.

    Stroke
    -Sudden weakness or numbness in the face, arm, or leg, especially on one side of the body
    -Sudden confusion, trouble speaking or understanding speech
    -Sudden trouble seeing in one or both eyes
    -Sudden trouble walking, dizziness, loss of balance or coordination
    -Sudden severe headache with no known cause
    -Nausea and vomiting
    -Loss of consciousness or passing out.

    Brain tumor
    -Headaches (often worse in the morning)
    -Nausea and vomiting
    -Seizures
    -Memory loss and confusion
    -Changes in vision, speech or hearing
    -Weakness or numbness in the arms or legs
    -Trouble with balance or coordination
    -Personality or mood changes
    -Increased pressure within the skull (seen as headache, nausea, vomiting, drowsiness, confusion, and in severe cases, unconsciousness)

    Spinal cord injury
    -Numbness or loss of sensation in the limbs
    -Weakness or partial paralysis of the limbs
    -Loss of bladder and/or bowel control
    -Severe back pain or pressure in the neck, head, or back
    -Spasms or muscle spasms
    -Changes in sexual function
    -Changes in reflexes
    -Difficulty breathing or coughing
    -Unusual sensations such as tingling or burning

    Restless Legs Syndrome (RLS)
    -Uncomfortable sensations in the legs, such as crawling, tingling, or burning, usually accompanied by an irresistible urge to move the legs.
    -Symptoms typically occur in the evening or nighttime and can interfere with sleep.
    -Relief from symptoms with movement, such as stretching, walking, or massaging the legs.
    -Increased symptoms during periods of rest or inactivity.
    -Symptoms can also occur in the arms and other parts of the body.
    -Excessive daytime sleepiness or fatigue.

  • OBSTETRICS
    • Ectopic pregnancy
    -Abnormal vaginal bleeding
    -Pelvic pain or discomfort, which may be severe
    -Shoulder pain (referred pain)
    -Weakness or fainting
    -Low blood pressure
    -Dizziness or lightheadedness
    -Abdominal pain or cramping
    -Nausea and vomiting
    -Constipation

    Miscarriage
    -Vaginal bleeding or spotting
    -Abdominal pain or cramping
    -Lower back pain
    -Passing of tissue or clots
    -Loss of pregnancy symptoms (such as morning sickness)

    Preterm labor
    -Contractions that occur regularly and become increasingly intense
    -Low back pain or pressure
    -Change in vaginal discharge (increased or thick and mucus-like)
    -Pelvic discomfort or a heavy, dull ache in the lower abdomen
    -Flu-like symptoms such as nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
    -A sense of pelvic pressure, like the baby is pushing down
    -A change in the baby's movement patterns
    -Uterine contractions that may or may not be painful

    Ovarian cyst.
    -Pelvic pain or discomfort, especially on one side of the pelvis
    -A full or bloated feeling in the lower abdomen
    -Pain or pressure during physical activity or intercourse
    -Nausea or vomiting
    -Breast tenderness
    -Irregular periods or heavy bleeding
    -Pain during bowel movements or urination

  • OPHTHALMOLOGY
    • Cataracts
    -Blurry or hazy vision
    -Sensitivity to light and glare
    -Difficulty with night vision
    -Double vision in one eye
    -Seeing "halos" around lights
    -Fading or yellowing of colors
    -Increasing nearsightedness
    -A need for brighter light for reading and other activities
    -Frequent changes in eyeglass or contact lens prescription.

    Glaucoma
    -Gradual loss of peripheral vision
    -Blurred vision
    -Halos around lights
    -Eye pain
    -Headache
    -Nausea and vomiting
    -Red eyes
    -Sudden vision loss in one eye

    Dry eye syndrome
    -Burning or stinging in the eyes
    -A sensation of having a foreign body in the eye
    -Pain or discomfort in the eye
    -Tearing or watery eyes
    -Blurred vision or sensitivity to light
    -Redness or inflammation of the eye
    -Heavy eyelids
    -A feeling of dryness, scratchiness, or sand in the eye.

    Strabismus (eye turn)
    -One eye that turns inward or outward while the other eye looks forward
    -Double vision in one eye or alternating between eyes
    -Eye fatigue or headaches when using one eye for prolonged periods
    -Tilting or tilting of the head to maintain single vision
    -Squinting one eye
    -Poor depth perception
    -Eye muscle weakness or amblyopia (lazy eye) in one eye.

    • Arthritis

    Osteoporosis

    Tendinitis

    Bursitis

    Fractures

    Dislocations

    Sprains and strains

    Tendinopathy

    Carpal tunnel syndrome

    Tennis elbow (lateral epicondylitis)

    Golfer's elbow (medial epicondylitis)

    Hip impingement

    Patellofemoral pain syndrome (runner's knee)

    Shin splints

    Plantar fasciitis

  • OTORHINOLARYNGOLOGY
    • Otitis media (ear infection)
    -Pain or pressure in the ear
    -Temporary hearing loss
    -Drainage from the ear

    Sinusitis
    -Pain or pressure in the sinuses
    -Nasal congestion
    -Headache
    -Fatigue

    Tonsillitis
    -Pain or soreness in the throat
    -Swollen tonsils
    -Difficulty swallowing
    -Fever

    Snoring
    -Excessive loud and disruptive snoring
    -Choking or gasping during sleep
    -Excessive daytime sleepiness

    Hearing loss
    -Gradual or sudden loss of hearing in one or both ears
    -Difficulty understanding speech
    -Tinnitus (ringing in the ears)

    Vertigo
    -Dizziness
    -Loss of balance
    -Nausea
    -Vomiting

    Sleep apnea
    -Snoring
    -Choking or gasping during sleep
    -Excessive daytime sleepiness
    -Restless sleep.
